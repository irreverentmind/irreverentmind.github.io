How Are You Supposed to BE?

Let's face it, most of us want other people to approve of us.  

So much so in fact, that we may trade being well thought-of, liked, esteemed, for a sense of just being ourselves.   

Sometimes we’re aware of this urge for good thoughts and inclusion, like when we obviously maneuver with bosses and colleagues, parents, spouse, kids, friends.  

More often though, approval-seeking is so pervasive that we don’t even notice our attempts to win over everyone, even strangers.

Think about how unsettling it is if a total stranger glances at you with obvious disapproval, or looks at you “funny" as you pass in the street, or doesn’t include you when you walk up to a group at a party.

You might begin to see that trying to please everyone is so frequent and so subtle that, realized or not… 

…It’s running our lives.

And yet, why? Why are people so concerned about the thoughts in
someone else’s head? 

And then you might begin to notice the masks designed to cover up the real, unappealing, unacceptable you. How much time is spent watching others, comparing yourself to see if you measure up. How alert you are to facial expressions and raised eyebrows.  Days or weeks can be ruined with stewing about some whispered comment – even if you don’t know for sure it had anything to do with you.

All that ruminating and strategizing trying to figure out what other people think…

about YOU.

What is it you really want from the hiding, faking, manipulating, lying, flattering, worrying, and apologizing?  And what is it that you get?  
 
Yes you’re not supposed to care about what they think.

But you do.  A lot.

Is this fun? Um, no.

So… How Much Longer?
How much more precious life are you willing to devote to what others think about you?
  • If you can relate to wanting to belong, to be well thought-of, liked, respected, despite being untrue to yourself
  • If you’re tired of spending so many of your waking hours trying to second guess what others are thinking about you
  • If you’re exhausted with the crazy-making self hate and misery that comes with all this…
Welcome to the online class Caring What People Think.
If at last enough is enough, this is the class for you.
Caring What People Think will be looking at:

 • Comparing
• Judging yourself and others
• Pretending, faking and hiding
• Keeping quiet
• Apologizing
• Flattering…

…. and what all this means about YOU.
